Today, every comp is typed by hand.

Lease comps are one of the most important data sets at Prologis — they inform what to buy and where. Today they arrive from every broker in every format, and a single analyst transcribes them one document at a time.

3–4KComps ingested per year — and the team wants that number to grow.
104Unique PDF & Excel template types, found auditing 229 docs over 7 months.
1 + 2One transcriber (Europe-based); two more sets of eyes before upload.
At capacityNo headroom — any volume increase is a direct risk to coverage.
The workflow today
1Comps arrive from brokersEmail, files — no upstream standardization across markets or brokers.
2Analyst reads & transcribes by handLooks at each document on screen and types fields into a standardized Excel.
3Two-person QA reviewTwo sets of eyes check accuracy before anything is trusted.
4Geocode & uploadMapped into the database, then geocoded for spatial analysis.
Why it's a bottleneck
  • Slow, inconsistent, not scalable. Manual transcription can't keep up with the volume the business actually wants.
  • Comps are formatted to resist reading — often deliberately. Many are hard even for the human eye.
  • No upstream standardization. Brokers won't (and largely can't) send a uniform template; the solution has to absorb the variety.
  • Accuracy & duplicates ride downstream. Investment decisions depend on this data being right and current.
  • Geocoding is rough — industrial addresses are often a street with no number; raw land has no address at all.

ChatGPT got 6% of the way there.

The team did the rigorous thing: built and deployed a custom GPT, then audited the whole document set. It worked — on a sliver — and proved exactly where the ceiling is.

What the team built
LeaseCompGPTDeployed
  • Custom ChatGPT for extraction → standardized Excel for database ingest.
  • Configured on 4 similar templates (JLL / CBRE / Lee / Voit, LA & IE).
  • 95% accurate on those — ~3 hours net time saved per doc, including QA.
  • But that set is only ~6% of volume.
Template audit — 229 docs, 104 unique types
Top 5 types31%
Top 10 types43%
The long tail57%
Why it hits a wall
  • Hallucinated values. Accuracy fell apart once trained past a few templates.
  • Black box. No auditability — you can't show why it decided what it decided.
  • No structured, repeatable output. Can't reliably enforce the schema the database needs.
  • No validation or correction workflow. Nothing native to catch, route, and fix the misses.
  • Can't be standardized away. Brokers won't change; a solution must handle variability, not eliminate it.
The team's own conclusion
A general-purpose LLM alone is not sufficient. To automate this you also need:
Handle a large range of templates Workflow on top of AI — not purely prompt-based Human-in-the-loop validation Learns from corrections over time

Not templates. A semantic layer.

Unframe doesn't learn your 104 templates — it learns what a lease comp is. So when a format changes, or a new broker shows up, nothing breaks. It's a workflow built on top of AI, not a prompt.

Deterministic, not purely prompt-based Not RAG chunking — preserves position, context & margin handwriting
